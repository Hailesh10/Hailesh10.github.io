About Me

I am a hydrologist and hydrogeologist with 10+ years of experience across academia, international development, and consulting. My research addresses water security under climate change through flood early warning systems (citizen science + satellite remote sensing + machine learning), coupled groundwater–surface water modelling (including heat transport for sub-arctic systems), and climate-resilient water governance translating science into policy across Africa, Europe, and the Middle East.

Currently a Postdoctoral Researcher at Aalto University (Finland), I develop fully coupled numerical models of groundwater–heat transport in sub-arctic riverbanks within Finland's national Digital Waters Flagship (DIWA). I aim to build an independent research group producing both fundamental advances and transferable decision-support tools for vulnerable communities.

Research Interests

  • Scientific machine learning & neural operators for hydrology
  • Flood early warning systems in data-scarce environments
  • Coupled groundwater–surface water & heat transport modelling
  • Remote sensing & citizen science data fusion
  • Climate-resilient water risk governance

Featured Projects

  • flood-ews-ml — Python pipeline integrating crowd-sourced gauges, MODIS/Sentinel-2, and HEC-HMS for ML-augmented flood forecasting (adopted by Ethiopian national disaster agency).
  • gw-sw-coupled — Coupled groundwater flow and heat transport model for sub-arctic riverbanks under freeze–thaw cycling.
  • satellite-hydrology — Remote sensing data fusion tools for water resources assessment.
